Documenting Major University Protests in Support of Palestine or Israel

Introduction

This interactive web map showcases the prominent universities across the United States and pinpoints locations where pro-Palestine and pro-Israel demonstrations have occurred. The map provides detailed information about major demonstrations on both sides, as documenting every protest instance proved impractical for this project. To identify significant protests, I considered two primary factors: the popularity of associated articles and the presence of multiple articles covering the event.

Limitations

As the sole contributor to this project, I encountered several limitations due to resource constraints. Firstly, not all major universities could be included. Secondly, sourcing reputable information for all university demonstrations proved challenging given the vast volume of articles on the subject. Lastly, reliance on available articles may have introduced potential inaccuracies into the data.

Conclusion

Among the protests I've documented, 40 have been in support of Palestine, while only 7 have supported Israel. This marks a significant 471% increase in pro-Palestinian demonstrations compared to those in support of Israel. These results were anticipated, given the prevailing political climate and media coverage on the topic. One interesting aspect I've observed is how university administrations have handled these protests. Some have opted for upholding the law, while others have engaged in dialogue with students. However, many officials have faced backlash for their public statements on the conflict, which has fueled support for Palestine. Understanding the political climate is crucial for fostering productive discussions. I hope that students, faculty, and community members can unite to condemn violence and collaborate on building a better future. Despite tensions between pro-Palestinian and pro-Israeli groups, we share many commonalities as humans. It's my belief that focusing on these shared values can help foster unity. The media only tries to polarize people and that often can prevent productive conversations.
